Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is one of the most celebrated cricketers of his generation. He rose to prominence as the captain of the Indian U-19 team that won the 2008 World Cup and made his international debut in ODIs later that year. Known for his aggressive batting style, Kohli quickly established himself as a key player, becoming one of the fastest to score over 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in ODIs. He captained the Indian Test team from 2014 and the limited-overs team from 2017, leading India to significant victories, including a historic Test series win in Australia in 2020-21. Off the field, Kohli is married to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, and they welcomed their daughter, Vamika, in January 2021. In addition to his cricketing achievements, he is known for his philanthropic efforts through the Virat Kohli Foundation, making a positive impact in various social causes. Kohli’s dedication, passion, and relentless pursuit of excellence have solidified his legacy as one of cricket's all-time greats.

Early Life

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi into a Punjabi Hindu family. His father, Prem Nath Kohli, worked as a criminal lawyer, and his mother, Saroj Kohli, served as a housewife. He has an older brother, Vikas, and an older sister, Bhawna.[9] Kohli's formative years were spent in Uttam Nagar. He commenced his early education at Vishal Bharti Public School.[10] According to his family, Kohli exhibited an early affinity for cricket as a three-year-old. He would pick up a cricket bat and request his father to bowl to him.

IPL career

Virat Kohli's IPL career is a remarkable testament to his talent and consistency in T20 cricket. He has been with the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) since the inaugural season in 2008, emerging as the team's linchpin and a fan favorite. Kohli has consistently been one of the top performers in the league, holding the record for the most runs scored in a single season, amassing 973 runs in 2016. As RCB's captain from 2013 to 2021, he showcased his leadership skills and aggressive playing style, often leading from the front with his bat. Despite not yet winning an IPL title, Kohli's explosive batting and ability to chase down targets have made him a formidable presence in the tournament, contributing significantly to its popularity. His passion for the game and commitment to excellence have solidified his status as one of the IPL's all-time greats.
